Questions We're Exploring

Living Experiments
01 ⚖️

Ownership Without Control

When everyone owns a thing equally, who decides? We're learning that ownership and decision-making authority are very different beasts.

02 🔧

Labor & Contribution

Two people did the mechanical work. Others paid in. Some bring snacks. How do groups account for unequal contributions without keeping score?

03 🤝

Trust as Infrastructure

There's no contract. No app. No legal entity. Just neighbors who said yes. Trust is doing all the work that institutions usually do.

04 📋

Emergent Rules

We made no rules upfront. Rules are emerging from situations we didn't anticipate. Is that a bug or a feature of organic governance?

05 💡

The Commons Problem

Hardin said the commons always collapses. Ostrom said it doesn't have to. We're 12 people about to find out who was right.

06 🌱

Can This Scale?

If a golf cart can teach 12 neighbors something real about shared ownership, what else could? A tool library? A garden? A neighborhood microgrid?

A Framework for Anything Owned by Everyone

Work in Progress

We're developing — through trial, error, and a surprising number of laughs — a working model for small-group shared ownership. It's rough, it's evolving, and we're writing it down as it happens.

Phase I

Acquire Together

Pool resources. Establish equity splits. Decide what "fair" means before you need to.

Phase II

Govern Informally

Resist the urge to formalize everything upfront. Let norms emerge from real situations.

Phase III

Handle Friction

Something will go wrong. That's the experiment. How you handle it is the data.

Phase IV

Document & Share

What you learn has value beyond your neighborhood. Write it down. Share it openly.
