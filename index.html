Manifesto

We didn't mean to start a movement.
We just wanted to ride around.

It started with a listing. Someone spotted it — a 10-seat golf cart that needed a little love. Twelve neighbors chipped in. Two of us rolled up our sleeves and did the real work of bringing it back to life. Nobody had a plan beyond "this will be fun."

But somewhere between splitting the bill and figuring out who keys the cart on a Tuesday evening, we started asking bigger questions. Questions that economists and political philosophers have wrestled with for centuries — wrapped in the humble packaging of a slow-moving vehicle.

"The real experiment isn't about the cart. It's about whether people who live near each other can build something together — and what happens when they try."

How do you make decisions as a group when there's no hierarchy? Who owns a problem when everyone owns the asset? What happens when two people did the hard mechanical work but everyone gets equal say — and what does fairness even mean when contributions aren't equal?

We're documenting all of it. The friction. The laughs. The governance debates nobody expected to have about a golf cart. Because we think the answers might matter beyond Coronado.

The Questions We're Exploring

Living Experiments
01 ⚖️

Ownership Without Control

When everyone owns a thing equally, who decides? We're learning that ownership and decision-making authority are very different beasts.

02 🔧

Labor & Contribution

Two people did the mechanical work. Others paid in. Some bring snacks. How do groups account for unequal contributions without keeping score?

03 🤝

Trust as Infrastructure

There's no contract. No app. No legal entity. Just neighbors who said yes. Trust is doing all the work that institutions usually do.

04 📋

Emergent Rules

We made no rules upfront. Rules are emerging from situations we didn't anticipate. Is that a bug or a feature of organic governance?

05 💡

The Commons Problem

Hardin said the commons always collapses. Ostrom said it doesn't have to. We're 12 people about to find out who was right.

06 🌱

Can This Scale?

If a golf cart can teach 12 neighbors something real about shared ownership, what else could? A tool library? A garden? A neighborhood microgrid?

A Framework for Anything
Owned by Everyone

We're developing — through trial, error, and a surprising amount of laughs — a working model for small-group shared ownership. It's rough. It's evolving. We're writing it down as it happens.

Phase I

Acquire Together

Pool resources. Establish equity splits. Decide what "fair" means before you need to.

Phase II

Govern Informally

Resist the urge to formalize everything upfront. Let norms emerge from real situations.

Phase III

Handle Friction

Something will go wrong. That's the experiment. How you handle it is the data.

Phase IV

Document & Share

What you learn has value beyond your neighborhood. Write it down. Share it.
